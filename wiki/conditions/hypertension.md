# Hypertension (Essential / Primary)

## Summary

Persistently elevated arterial blood pressure, in most adults with no single
identifiable secondary cause (essential/primary hypertension). A leading
modifiable risk factor for stroke, ischaemic heart disease, heart failure, and
chronic kidney disease. Diagnosis is based on repeated, correctly measured
office readings supported where possible by out-of-office measurement.

## Current Guideline

- Confirm the diagnosis on at least two separate occasions before labelling a
  patient hypertensive, unless BP is very high or end-organ damage is present.
- Stage thresholds (office BP, adults): **Stage 1** 140–159 / 90–99 mmHg;
  **Stage 2** ≥ 160 / ≥ 100 mmHg.
- General office target: **< 140/90 mmHg**; consider a tighter target
  (**< 130/80 mmHg**) in patients with diabetes, established cardiovascular
  disease, or chronic kidney disease, where tolerated.
- Start with lifestyle modification for all patients. Initiate drug therapy
  immediately alongside lifestyle change in Stage 2, or in Stage 1 with high
  cardiovascular risk or end-organ damage.
- Prefer a single daily combination regimen for Stage 2 rather than maximising
  a single agent.

## Diagnostic Criteria

- Office BP ≥ 140/90 mmHg on two or more occasions, using a validated,
  correctly sized cuff after 5 minutes of rest, arm supported at heart level.
- Where available, corroborate with home BP averaging ≥ 135/85 mmHg.
- Assess for secondary causes when onset is young (< 30 yr), resistant to
  three drugs, or accompanied by suggestive features (e.g., hypokalaemia,
  abdominal bruit).
- Baseline workup: fasting glucose/HbA1c, lipid profile, serum
  creatinine/eGFR, electrolytes, urinalysis for protein, and a 12-lead ECG.

## Treatment

- **Lifestyle (all patients):** salt restriction (< 5 g/day), weight reduction,
  the DASH-style diet, regular aerobic activity, alcohol moderation, smoking
  cessation.
- **First-line drug classes:** ACE inhibitor or ARB, calcium-channel blocker
  (e.g., `[[drugs/amlodipine]]`), or thiazide/thiazide-like diuretic.
- **Stage 1, low risk:** single agent, titrate.
- **Stage 2 or BP > 20/10 above target:** start two-drug combination
  (commonly ACEi/ARB + CCB).
- **Step-up:** if uncontrolled, move to a three-drug combination (ACEi/ARB +
  CCB + diuretic) before labelling resistant hypertension.
- Reassess adherence and technique at every step before escalating.

## Drug Interactions

- Do **not** combine an ACE inhibitor with an ARB (increased risk of
  hyperkalaemia, hypotension, renal dysfunction).
- ACEi/ARB + potassium-sparing diuretic or potassium supplements → monitor
  potassium.
- NSAIDs blunt the antihypertensive effect of most agents and worsen renal
  function when combined with ACEi/ARB + diuretic ("triple whammy").
- Non-dihydropyridine CCBs (verapamil, diltiazem) + beta-blocker → risk of
  bradycardia and heart block.

## Red Flags

- BP ≥ 180/120 mmHg with signs of acute end-organ damage (chest pain,
  breathlessness, focal neurology, visual loss, acute kidney injury) →
  hypertensive emergency, refer urgently.
- New severe headache, confusion, or seizures.
- Suspected secondary or resistant hypertension warranting specialist referral.
- Pregnancy: avoid ACEi/ARB; manage per obstetric hypertension pathway.
