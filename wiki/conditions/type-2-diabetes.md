# Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

## Summary

A chronic metabolic disorder of insulin resistance and progressive beta-cell
dysfunction causing hyperglycaemia. Highly prevalent in the Indian population,
often presenting at a lower BMI and younger age than in Western cohorts.
Management targets glycaemic control plus aggressive cardiovascular risk
reduction to prevent micro- and macrovascular complications.

## Current Guideline

- Screen at-risk adults (central obesity, family history, hypertension,
  prior gestational diabetes) even when asymptomatic.
- Individualise the **HbA1c target**: ~**< 7%** for most non-pregnant adults;
  relax toward 7.5–8% in the elderly, those with limited life expectancy, or
  recurrent hypoglycaemia.
- **Metformin** is first-line pharmacotherapy unless contraindicated, started
  alongside structured lifestyle change.
- Add agents with proven cardiorenal benefit (SGLT2 inhibitor or GLP-1 receptor
  agonist) early in patients with established ASCVD, heart failure, or CKD.
- Treat blood pressure and lipids concurrently; most patients need a statin.

## Diagnostic Criteria

Any one of the following (confirm asymptomatic results on a repeat sample):

- Fasting plasma glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L).
- 2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL during a 75 g OGTT.
- HbA1c ≥ 6.5%.
- Random plasma glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL with classic symptoms (polyuria,
  polydipsia, unexplained weight loss).

Prediabetes: FPG 100–125 mg/dL, HbA1c 5.7–6.4%, or 2-hr OGTT 140–199 mg/dL.

## Treatment

- **Lifestyle:** medical nutrition therapy, ≥ 150 min/week of activity, weight
  loss of 5–10% where overweight, tobacco cessation.
- **First-line:** metformin, titrated to tolerance.
- **Second-line (add-on, individualised):** SGLT2 inhibitor or GLP-1 RA
  (preferred with ASCVD/HF/CKD), sulfonylurea or DPP-4 inhibitor (cost-sensitive
  settings), pioglitazone.
- **Insulin:** for marked hyperglycaemia (e.g., HbA1c > 10%, symptomatic),
  catabolic features, or failure of oral therapy.
- Complication surveillance: annual retinal exam, foot exam, urine
  albumin-to-creatinine ratio, eGFR, and lipid profile.

## Drug Interactions

- Metformin: withhold around iodinated contrast and acute illness with risk of
  hypoxia/dehydration (lactic acidosis risk); avoid if eGFR < 30.
- Sulfonylureas + alcohol or beta-blockers → masked or prolonged
  hypoglycaemia.
- SGLT2 inhibitor + loop diuretic → additive volume depletion.
- Many agents interact with concurrent corticosteroids (hyperglycaemia) — review
  steroid courses.

## Red Flags

- Suspected diabetic ketoacidosis or hyperosmolar hyperglycaemic state
  (vomiting, deep breathing, altered sensorium, very high glucose) → emergency.
- Hypoglycaemia with impaired consciousness.
- Diabetic foot with spreading infection, deep ulcer, or gangrene → urgent
  referral.
- Rapidly worsening vision or new proteinuria/declining eGFR.
