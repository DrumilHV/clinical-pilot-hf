# Dengue Fever

## Summary

An acute mosquito-borne viral illness (DENV, *Aedes aegypti*) endemic across
India, with seasonal post-monsoon peaks. Ranges from a self-limiting febrile
illness to dengue with warning signs and severe dengue (plasma leakage,
haemorrhage, organ impairment). Management is supportive; early recognition of
warning signs is critical.

## Current Guideline

- Use the WHO classification: dengue without warning signs, dengue with warning
  signs, and severe dengue.
- Manage most cases as outpatients with oral fluids, paracetamol, and daily
  review; admit those with warning signs or risk factors.
- Monitor haematocrit and platelet trends — a rising haematocrit signals plasma
  leakage.
- **Avoid NSAIDs and aspirin** (bleeding risk); use paracetamol for fever.

## Diagnostic Criteria

- Acute febrile illness with ≥ 2 of: headache, retro-orbital pain, myalgia,
  arthralgia, rash, leukopenia, or any warning sign, in an endemic area.
- Laboratory: NS1 antigen (early, days 1–5), or dengue IgM/IgG (from ~day 5);
  PCR where available.
- Warning signs: abdominal pain/tenderness, persistent vomiting, clinical fluid
  accumulation, mucosal bleeding, lethargy, hepatomegaly, rising haematocrit
  with rapid platelet fall.

## Treatment

- **Without warning signs:** oral rehydration, paracetamol, rest; daily CBC and
  review; advise return for any warning sign.
- **With warning signs / severe:** admit; judicious isotonic IV fluids titrated
  to haematocrit and urine output; monitor closely for shock.
- Transfuse only for significant bleeding; platelet transfusion is **not**
  indicated for thrombocytopenia alone without bleeding.
- Watch for the critical (defervescence) phase around days 3–7.

## Drug Interactions

- Avoid NSAIDs/aspirin and intramuscular injections (bleeding/haematoma risk).
- Caution with paracetamol dosing in hepatic involvement (dengue hepatitis).
- Anticoagulant/antiplatelet therapy increases bleeding risk — review and adjust
  with specialist input.

## Red Flags

- Any warning sign (severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, bleeding,
  lethargy) → admit.
- Signs of shock: cold clammy extremities, narrow pulse pressure, hypotension,
  reduced urine output → emergency.
- Severe plasma leakage, respiratory distress, severe bleeding, or organ
  impairment (AST/ALT ≥ 1000, altered sensorium) → severe dengue, escalate.
